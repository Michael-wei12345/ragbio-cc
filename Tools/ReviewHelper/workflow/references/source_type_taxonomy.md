# Source type taxonomy

`Source type` is access/extraction status: `Full-text PDF`, `PMC/full-text HTML`, `Metadata/abstract only`, `Landing-page snippets`, `Inaccessible`, `Duplicate`, or `Superseded`.

`Type of source` is publication/study design. Prefer these controlled values:

- `Primary study - randomized controlled trial`
- `Primary study - nonrandomized trial`
- `Primary study - cohort study`
- `Primary study - case-control study`
- `Primary study - cross-sectional study`
- `Primary study - diagnostic accuracy study`
- `Primary study - case series`
- `Primary study - qualitative study`
- `Case report - supplementary only`
- `Systematic review - background only`
- `Meta-analysis - background only`
- `Narrative review - background only`
- `Scoping review - background only`
- `Guideline - background only`
- `Consensus statement - background only`
- `Editorial/commentary - background only`
- `Letter - verify original data`
- `Protocol - no outcomes`
- `Preprint - classify by design`
- `Conference abstract - limited data`
- `Book chapter - background only`
- `Other/unclear - verify full text`

For a standard intervention, exposure, prognosis, diagnosis, prevalence, or outcomes SR, final included studies should normally be primary research reporting original participants, specimens, records, or images. Background-only sources can inform context but must not contribute participant denominators or pooled effects. Mark a preprint superseded when a peer-reviewed version exists.
